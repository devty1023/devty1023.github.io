finishing cis194

Posted on June 29, 2014 by devty

For my introduction into the world of functional programming, I’ve been relying on the course webpage for UPenn’s CIS194: Introduction to Haskell. It’s really a fine collection of articles and exercises curated by Brent Yorgey. Pretty awesome guy - I need to make sure I reach out to him and send him my appreciation when I finish his online modules.

I just finished the modules for week 10 and 11 this weekend and will probably conclude the course with a module on Monads by the end of this week. It’s been almost an addictive ride - when was I ever excited to dive into the homework set to see what treat the instructor had prepared for me this week? Sometimes, I would even go forward to the next module even though I needed to spend more time grasping the current one - “Eh I’m not sure if I intuitively understand functors…but wait I gotta read these articles about applicative functors!!!” Pretty nerdy stuff.

I think Haskell really struck chords with me because it would not stop impressing me with wonderful concepts it encompasses. It was like the abstract algebra or discrete mathematics course I had really enjoyed back in college - unending presentation of some beautiful concepts that one had never imagined existed - but even more excellent because I can physically play with the idea using my handy dandy command line. What a treat it was - a thousand shout-out to Brent for his awesome exercises.

For the course’s final assignment, Brent gives few options to the students, one of the being making a non-trivial contribution to an open source project. I actually have been wanting to do exactly that for a while and had in my to make a contribution to hakyll, the blogging platform that powers this website. Hakyll itself is made to be ultra-configurable, and it seemed to me that the consensus of the its user-base is to keep it that way - in other words, the contributions they seek probably aren’t some feature candy for the platform. But I do wish there were more batteries that comes included with the platform, or at least a central place where people share some of the nifty features they had set up for their hakyll-based website. Ideas..ideas..

Anyhow, what an entertainment it was to go through CIS194. My solutions to the exercises lives in github, although it might be pulled down if Brent does not want it public (because he should definitely recycle his awesomes exercises). Back to reading the Typeclassopedia and reading about Monads to conclude the course!
